You look in despair at the box sitting on your desk. Despite weeks of meticulous planning, all attempts at breathing life into the machine have failed. It taunts you with silence.

But you mustn't give up! This computer cost far too much in effort and money to simply abandon. So you decide to make one final attempt, against all odds.

Continue...
Back...

Relinquished of your previous doubts, you plug in the monitor, and press the power button.

  • The monitor flickers to life.
  • No signal. Damn.
  • The monitor is blank and expressionless.
Back...

You power off, and open the side panel to get a closer look at the poor wretch. Once again, you press the power button.

  • All the fans lie lifeless. You hear crickets chirping outside.
  • The fans spin up for a while, then shut off.
  • The fans spin up as normal.
Back...

In a sudden stroke of genius, you decide to buy a motherboard speaker, and so set off on a trek to the local electronics shop.

  • You return triumphant, speaker in hand, and thirsty (the electronics shop is quite a way away).
  • You fool! You walked all this way despite already owning a motherboard speaker.
  • Getting a speaker was a terrible idea. You head home, ashamed by your terrible decision making skills.
Back...

You install the speaker in the PC, and press the power button a third time. The motherboard starts making strange beeping noises, as if it's trying to communicate with you. You read the manual to try and understand what it means.

  • There's some issue with the graphics (VGA).
  • There's an problem with the memory.
  • It wants you to plug in a keyboard.
  • You must have been hearing things, because the motherboard didn't make any sounds.
Back...

You see some text appear on the screen.

  • It's complaining about RAM. You wonder why the computer cares about sheep.
  • It's complaining about the boot device. You wonder if that's some kind of shoehorn.
  • It's complaining about power. You wonder who's coming up with these "jokes".

Are you absolutely sure it's plugged in?

Yes.

Plug in a keyboard!

Done.
Back...

All of a sudden, you have a brainwave, in which all of the following suggestions pass through your mind:

  • Check the CPU power cable is plugged in (4pin).
  • Check the CPU fan is plugged in.
  • Check the CPU for broken pins.
  • Try reseating the CPU / reapplying thermal paste.

  • Make sure the monitor isn't plugged into the motherboard (if you have a graphics card).
  • Check the GPU PCIe power cable(s) is/are plugged in.
  • Try reseating the GPU.

  • Try reseating the memory.
  • Check you have the memory in the correct slot(s) (RTFM).
  • If you have spare RAM slots, use those instead.

  • Is the PSU plugged in and switched on?
  • Check all the power cables are connected (including the 12pin ATX cable).
  • Try the paper-clip test on the PSU.

  • Is the hard drive plugged in? Does it show up in the BIOS?
  • Is there a disc drive with a bootable disc also plugged in?
  • Check the hard drive's power cable is plugged in.
  • Try booting into Linux using a DVD, and examining the contents of the HDD.
  • If the HDD is making clicking / grinding sounds it may be on its way out. If it's a new drive, consider an RMA.

  • Check you haven't installed any superfluous motherboard stand-offs.
  • Is the 12pin ATX power cable installed correctly?

Well done, you found this feature Easter egg!
